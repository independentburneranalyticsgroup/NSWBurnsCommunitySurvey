Who We Are

Before we explore our future, we need to understand who we are right now. The data shows we are not starting from scratch; we are an established community of adults with deep experience, resources, and complex responsibilities. This is our strength.

Our community's core is middle-aged (73% are 35-54), indicating a stable, experienced participant base.

Experience Level

We are a veteran-heavy community. A remarkable 83% have attended multiple Burner events, with over 60% having been to five or more.

This data reveals both a strength and a challenge: with 61% of us having attended 5+ events, how do we create conscious pathways for newcomers, so we don't risk becoming a closed circle?

Our Culture & Values

What truly defines us? It's not just what we do, but *why* we do it. The data here reveals our core motivations and uncovers a profound truth about our community's makeup that changes everything.

Primary Motivations

Why do we come? Overwhelmingly, for community and connection, and self-expression.

Key Motivators:

  • ➤
    Community & Connection (76%): Drawn to the strong, inclusive community ethos.
    "It's always about community..."
  • ➤
    Self-Expression & Creativity (62%): A non-judgmental space for authentic self-expression.
    "Being able to be myself and drop the mask..."

This was the finding that changed everything. At triple the general population rate, we are not a neurotypical community with a neurodivergent minority. We are a neurodivergent-majority community. This means we're not just accommodating a few; we must design for our majority.

A Neurodivergent-Majority Community

With 58% of our community identifying as or exploring neurodivergence, we are fundamentally different from the general population. This demands we prioritise sensory-friendly spaces and clear communication.

Accessibility is Inclusion

A massive 77% of our community benefits from accessibility features. The greatest needs are sensory and physical, making accessibility a core design principle.

Capacity & Leadership

Our Capabilities

We have the people and the skills to create amazing things. But *how* we engage that talent is our biggest challenge. The data here shows both our incredible capacity and the signs of critical burnout.

How We Contribute

Participation is our foundation. A significant number take on key leadership and creative roles that make events possible.

Community Skillset

Our community possesses a deep well of practical skills, with a healthy mix of members ready to lead teams and others happy to contribute their expertise.

This is both our greatest strength and our most critical vulnerability. We are a community of dedicated contributors, but we are at risk of consuming our most dedicated people.

Volunteer Experience

Our community has deep experience across all essential roles, demonstrating a proven commitment to our principles.

Leadership Interest

There is a healthy balance of people ready to lead and support. 51% are interested in coordination roles, suggesting a sustainable pipeline for future events.

Preferences & Vision

Future Vision

So where do we go from here? The data shows the community isn't divided; it's sophisticated. There is a clear and nuanced vision for a hybrid future that balances large-scale spectacle with intimate, regular connection.

We've lost our big Burn, communication is scattered, and volunteers are struggling. But the data shows we know exactly what we want. This isn't about mourning what was; it's about building what our community actually needs.

At a Glance: Community Priorities

This chart consolidates several key questions to provide a clear overview of what the community values most. Building a strong local community and ensuring accessibility are paramount.

Monthly Event Likelihood

The shoulder seasons of Autumn (Mar-Apr) and Spring (Oct-Nov) are clear favourites, avoiding peak summer fire risk and deep winter cold.

Preferred Long Weekend

The October Labour Day long weekend is the top choice, aligning with the monthly preference data. However, with 43% stating any long weekend works, there is huge flexibility.

Travel Willingness by Event Size

Willingness to travel increases significantly with event size. The sweet spot for a mini-burn is up to 4 hours, while for larger burns, 6 hours or more is widely accepted.

Evidence-Based Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive analysis of community data, the following strategic recommendations provide a clear path forward.

1. For Leadership & Governance

Establish formal governance structures with defined roles and transparent processes.

Rationale: Addresses widespread calls for improved structure, transparency, and a healthier leadership culture.

Implement a central communication platform to serve as a single source of truth.

Rationale: Reduces reliance on Facebook, which was cited as a major failure and cause of information silos.

2. For Event & Site Planning

Adopt a hybrid event portfolio (one large burn, multiple smaller events).

Rationale: Satisfies the split community vision, balancing the need for scale with the desire for intimacy and accessibility.

Design all events with neurodivergent accessibility as a core principle.

Rationale: With 58% of the community identifying as or exploring neurodivergence, this is essential design, not minority accommodation.

3. For Community Resources & Partnerships

Develop a community-wide, opt-in directory of skills and resources.

Rationale: Leverages the community's vast internal assets and connects creators with the talent they need.

Establish a dedicated community relations role to manage external relationships.

Rationale: Essential for securing sites, ensuring compliance, and building long-term goodwill as events grow.

A Community Ready to Build

The data from 518 community members paints a clear and powerful picture: the NSW Burns community is mature, experienced, and brimming with the capacity for sustainable growth. This is not a community searching for a direction; it is one with a clear vision, proven capabilities, and the collective will to turn its aspirations into reality.

With a strong contingent of veteran participants, a vast pool of willing volunteers, and a deep-seated commitment to core values like radical inclusion and creative expression, the foundation is solid. The path forward, as illuminated by this data, is not just possible—it is probable. The necessary leadership, skills, and passion will emerge from within, because 500 committed burners create their own momentum.
