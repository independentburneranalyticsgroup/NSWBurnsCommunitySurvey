Portraits of Our Community

Who We Are

This section provides a snapshot of our community's demographics. The data reveals a mature, experienced, and geographically concentrated group with deep roots in Burner culture, ready to guide its next chapter.

Age Distribution

Our community's core is middle-aged (73% are 35-54), indicating a stable, experienced participant base with the life stability and resources for long-term community building.

Geographic Distribution

Our community is firmly rooted in NSW, but with significant interstate appeal, providing a strong foundation for local events with broader reach.

Experience Level

We are a veteran-heavy community. A remarkable 83% have attended multiple Burner events, with over 60% having been to five or more. This deep cultural knowledge is our greatest asset for maintaining authenticity and mentoring newcomers.

Family & Kids' Participation

A significant portion of our community are parents, and many are open to or actively bring their children to events, highlighting the need for family-friendly spaces and planning.

Parents in the Community

21% of respondents have children under 18 in their care.

Bringing Kids to Events

Of those with kids, a combined 66% have brought them or are open to it, while 27% prefer to attend without them.

A Neurodivergent-Majority Community

With 58% of our community identifying as or exploring neurodivergence, we are fundamentally different from the general population. This has profound implications for event design, demanding we prioritise sensory-friendly spaces, clear communication, and flexible participation.

Primary Motivations

Why do we come? Overwhelmingly, for community and connection, and self-expression. This tells us that successful events must prioritise participation and authentic human interaction over passive entertainment.

Key Motivators:

  • ➤
    Community & Connection (76%): Participants are drawn to the strong, inclusive community ethos.
    "It's always about community and have a Preference for events where the community is strong and at the heart."
  • ➤
    Self-Expression & Creativity (62%): Events provide a non-judgmental space for authentic self-expression.
    "Being able to be myself and drop the mask I have to ware in a nerotypical world and not be judged."
  • ➤
    Personal Growth & Transformation (42%): Many see these events as "transformational" and a place to live as an example of how they want to be in the world.

Accessibility is Inclusion

While 20% face physical challenges, a massive 77% of our community benefits from accessibility features. The greatest needs are sensory: sound separation and quiet zones. This makes accessibility a core design principle, not an afterthought.

Capacity & Leadership

Volunteer Experience

Leadership Interest

Preferences & Vision

For Organisers

What does the future look like? This section details the community's preferences for event timing, size, and format, providing a clear, evidence-based roadmap for event organisers to follow.

At a Glance: Community Priorities

This chart consolidates several key questions to provide a clear overview of what the community values most. It's clear that building a strong local community and ensuring accessibility are paramount, far outweighing concerns about official status or event timing.

Monthly Event Likelihood

The shoulder seasons of Autumn (Mar-Apr) and Spring (Oct-Nov) are clear favourites, avoiding the peak summer fire risk and deep winter cold. The community shows strong preference for events outside of January and the mid-winter months.

Preferred Long Weekend

The October Labour Day long weekend is the top choice, aligning with the monthly preference data. However, with 43% stating any long weekend works, there is huge flexibility for a year-round calendar of events.

Travel Willingness by Event Size

Willingness to travel increases significantly with event size. The sweet spot for a mini-burn is up to 4 hours. For larger, destination-style burns, a travel time of 6 hours or more is widely accepted, greatly expanding the potential search area for a site.

Future Vision & Community Development

The following sections provide a qualitative analysis of the community's current state, identifying key strengths to build upon, critical gaps to address, and a shared vision for the future.

What's Working Well? (Strengths)

Despite organisational challenges, community cohesion is being actively maintained through smaller-scale events and strong interpersonal connections. The community demonstrates remarkable resilience and adaptability.

  • Small Burns & Local Events (32%): Highly valued for accessibility. "Small regional events are amazing."
  • Community Connection (24%): Strong interpersonal bonds persist. "United community- theme camps coming back to do awesome stuff together."
  • Events & Activities (20%): Regular social gatherings maintain engagement. "Frequency of events - the in between things like burning pub and fundraisers."

What's Missing? (Gaps)

Significant gaps exist in communication, event structure, and culture. There is a strong desire for more robust systems, a clearer event strategy, and a more inclusive, transparent culture.

  • Communication & Connection (24%): Facebook dependence creates information silos. "Using Facebook Pages as a primary online hub is a poor substitute for a moderated forum."
  • Community Culture & Inclusivity (20%): Exclusionary dynamics are affecting cohesion. "More inclusion and less 'cool camp' vibes."
  • Governance & Transparency (12%): Need for clearer decision-making. "We could always improve on transparency, accountability and accessibility."

What is Our Vision for the Future?

The community's vision is overwhelmingly positive, centred on fostering a more connected, creative, and inclusive environment. The underlying desire is for a "unified community that grows, trusts, and supports each other."

  • Community Building & Connection (36%): The foremost desire is for strengthened interpersonal bonds.
  • Event Size & Location (28%): A sophisticated preference for a hybrid model of large and small events.
  • Art & Creative Expression (16%): Art remains central to the community's identity.

Deeper Insights: Challenges & Opportunities

This section explores the detailed feedback from key contributor groups within our community. The insights reveal common themes around sustainability, communication, and the need for better structural support to prevent burnout and foster creativity.

Enhancing the Volunteer Experience

Volunteers seek significant improvements in structure, communication, and recognition. There's a strong desire for shorter, manageable shifts and better support systems to prevent burnout.

  • Shift Duration & Workload (32%): "Not having too long shifts during the event, max 4 hours at a time."
  • Training & Development (28%): "Training for bigger roles... Training off site and support on site."
  • Role Clarity & Communication (26%): "More clarity on other people's roles, clarity somewhat on my own role."
  • Recognition & Rewards (16%): "Great Schwag and recognition."

Theme Camp Challenges

Theme camps are under significant strain from workload, financial pressures, and logistics, indicating the current support model is leading to burnout.

  • Workload & Resource Distribution (42%): "Core crew doing 80% of the work."
  • Financial Challenges (36%): "Unsustainably low grant amounts."
  • Logistics & Transportation (30%): "Transport for all the gear is a constant battle."
  • Interpersonal Dynamics (24%): "POLITICS. OMG again and again I have seen dreadful behaviour."

Artist Challenges

Artists face significant funding limitations and logistical complexities, leading to personal financial strain that may limit artistic participation.

  • Funding & Costs (35%): "The costs easily go over grant budget."
  • Transportation & Logistics (32%): "Logistics to move heavy items."
  • Site Placement & Installation (24%): "Site placement has been a mess every time."
  • Safety & Durability (16%): "Not understanding how rough people will be with it."

Leadership Sustainability

Leaders desire more formalised processes, clearer documentation, and a more collaborative approach to enable sustainable community building and prevent burnout.

  • Communication & Documentation (24%): "Clarity of communication especially deadlines and requirements."
  • Organisational Structure (20%): "Standardized processes. Simple doco for expected tasks and deadlines."
  • Volunteer Management (18%): "An easy way for volunteers to sign up for shifts."
  • Leadership Culture (16%): "Get rid of the 'Important people'."

Evidence-Based Recommendations

Based on the comprehensive analysis of community data, the following strategic recommendations provide a clear path forward.

1. For Leadership & Governance

Establish formal governance structures with defined roles and transparent processes.

Rationale: Addresses widespread calls for improved structure, transparency, and a healthier leadership culture.

Implement a central communication platform to serve as a single source of truth.

Rationale: Reduces reliance on Facebook, which was cited as a major failure and cause of information silos.

Develop a formal leadership Code of Conduct and provide training opportunities.

Rationale: Responds directly to concerns about "egos," negative culture, and provides a framework for accountability.

2. For Event & Site Planning

Adopt a hybrid event portfolio (one large burn, multiple smaller events).

Rationale: Satisfies the split community vision, balancing the need for scale with the desire for intimacy and accessibility.

Prioritise site locations based on travel time appropriate to event size.

Rationale: Aligns with travel willingness data, maximising attendance by placing smaller burns closer to population centres.

Design all events with neurodivergent accessibility as a core principle.

Rationale: With 58% of the community identifying as or exploring neurodivergence, this is essential design, not minority accommodation.

3. For Community Resources & Partnerships

Develop a community-wide, opt-in directory of skills and resources.

Rationale: Leverages the community's vast internal assets and connects creators with the talent they need.

Establish a dedicated community relations role to manage external relationships.

Rationale: Essential for securing sites, ensuring compliance, and building long-term goodwill as events grow.

A Community Ready to Build

The data from 518 community members paints a clear and powerful picture: the NSW Burns community is mature, experienced, and brimming with the capacity for sustainable growth. This is not a community searching for a direction; it is one with a clear vision, proven capabilities, and the collective will to turn its aspirations into reality.

With a strong contingent of veteran participants, a vast pool of willing volunteers, and a deep-seated commitment to core values like radical inclusion and creative expression, the foundation is solid. The path forward, as illuminated by this data, is not just possible—it is probable. The necessary leadership, skills, and passion will emerge from within, because 500 committed burners create their own momentum.
