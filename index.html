Portraits of Our Community

Who We Are

This section provides a snapshot of our community's demographics. The data reveals a mature, experienced, and geographically concentrated group with deep roots in Burner culture, ready to guide its next chapter.

Age Distribution

Our community's core is middle-aged (73% are 35-54), indicating a stable, experienced participant base with the life stability and resources for long-term community building.

Geographic Distribution

Our community is firmly rooted in NSW, but with significant interstate appeal, providing a strong foundation for local events with broader reach.

Experience Level

We are a veteran-heavy community. A remarkable 83% have attended multiple Burner events, with over 60% having been to five or more. This deep cultural knowledge is our greatest asset for maintaining authenticity and mentoring newcomers.

Family & Kids' Participation

A significant portion of our community are parents, and many are open to or actively bring their children to events, highlighting the need for family-friendly spaces and planning.

Parents in the Community

21% of respondents have children under 18 in their care.

Bringing Kids to Events

Of those with kids, a combined 66% have brought them or are open to it, while 27% prefer to attend without them.

Inclusion & Connection

Our Values

This section explores the 'why' behind our community. The data on neurodiversity, accessibility, and motivation reveals a deep-seated need for authentic connection, self-expression, and spaces that are genuinely inclusive for all.

A Neurodivergent-Majority Community

With 58% of our community identifying as or exploring neurodivergence, we are fundamentally different from the general population. This has profound implications for event design, demanding we prioritise sensory-friendly spaces, clear communication, and flexible participation.

Primary Motivations

Why do we come? Overwhelmingly, for community and connection (76%), and self-expression (62%). This tells us that successful events must prioritise participation and authentic human interaction over passive entertainment.

Accessibility is Inclusion

While 20% face physical challenges, a massive 77% of our community benefits from accessibility features. The greatest needs are sensory: sound separation and quiet zones. This makes accessibility a core design principle, not an afterthought.

Capacity & Leadership

Our Skills

This section showcases our collective strength. The data reveals an abundance of volunteer experience and leadership potential. We don't have a problem of scarcity; we have an opportunity of abundance, with the right people ready to step up.

How We Contribute

Participation is our foundation. The majority contribute as Theme Camp members (66%) and Event Volunteers (62%), forming the vibrant, creative core of our events. A significant number also take on leadership as Theme Camp organisers (30%) and Art Creators (24%).

Volunteer Experience

Our community has deep experience across all essential roles, demonstrating a proven commitment to our principles and the safety and enjoyment of all participants.

Leadership Interest

There is a healthy balance of people ready to lead and support. 51% are interested in coordination roles, with a strong preference for project-based leadership, suggesting a sustainable pipeline for future events.

Preferences & Vision

The Path Forward

What does the future look like? This section details the community's preferences for event timing, size, and format, providing a clear, evidence-based roadmap for event organisers to follow.

What Matters Most?

Building a strong, local NSW community is the overwhelming priority. Accessibility for Sydney participants is also key. Interstate connections are valued, but secondary to fostering the local scene.

Official Status Importance

The community is split, but a majority (54%) feel official Burning Man Regional status is not important. This suggests success is not seen as dependent on official affiliation, empowering local organisers.

Monthly Event Likelihood

The shoulder seasons of Autumn (Mar-Apr) and Spring (Oct-Nov) are clear favourites, avoiding the peak summer fire risk and deep winter cold. The community shows strong preference for events outside of January and the mid-winter months.

Importance of Public Holidays

The community is divided on the need for events to be on public holidays. While 38% see it as important, a nearly equal 34% do not, and 27% are neutral. This gives organisers flexibility in scheduling.

Preferred Long Weekend

The October Labour Day long weekend is the top choice, aligning with the monthly preference data. However, with 43% stating any long weekend works, there is huge flexibility for a year-round calendar of events.

Importance of Large-Scale Art Burns

The Effigy or Temple burn is a cornerstone of the experience for many. Over half the community (51%) considers it a very important or essential part of the event, while 42% see it as a nice feature but not critical.

Collective Wisdom

Community Resources

The survey unearthed a treasure trove of actionable leads, from potential event sites to skilled suppliers. This is a community with the connections and resources to make its vision a reality.

Finding a New Home

  • Top Suggestions: Glenworth Valley, Dragon Dreaming site (Wee Jasper), Confest site (Woorooma Station).
  • Other Mentions: Matong State Forest, Castle Mountain, Parkes, Wingham/Taree area, Riverwood Downs.
  • Actionable Leads: Contacts provided for properties in Bellingen, Mandalong, and "The Lair" on the Central Coast.

Community Suppliers & Equipment

  • Sound/AV: Cody Lawton (Litness Lights), Justin "Cosmos" James (Dynamic Audio Visual), Julian Lacey/Nicholas Randell (Sunset Town).
  • Maker Spaces: Newcastle's Sparkhaus and Sydney's RoboDino.
  • Logistics: Sapphire Events, Spaceman Design, ATS Logistics, Crew on Call.

Support for Artists & Theme Camps

  • Primary Need: Increased and more accessible funding, including transport subsidies.
  • Shared Resources: Desire for a community "Library of Things" for tools and equipment.
  • Mentorship: A structured system for knowledge sharing between experienced and new creators.

Potential Community Partners

  • Festivals: ConFest, Dragon Dreaming, Modifyre (QLD), Blazing Swan (WA).
  • Arts/Maker Groups: Sparkhaus, local Men's Sheds, Newtown Festival.
  • Government/Local Bodies: Local Aboriginal Land Councils, Regional Arts NSW, Create NSW.
  • Environmental Groups: Reverse Garbage, The Bower, Landcare.

A Community Ready to Build

The data from 518 community members paints a clear and powerful picture: the NSW Burns community is mature, experienced, and brimming with the capacity for sustainable growth. This is not a community searching for a direction; it is one with a clear vision, proven capabilities, and the collective will to turn its aspirations into reality.

With a strong contingent of veteran participants, a vast pool of willing volunteers, and a deep-seated commitment to core values like radical inclusion and creative expression, the foundation is solid. The path forward, as illuminated by this data, is not just possible—it is probable. The necessary leadership, skills, and passion will emerge from within, because 500 committed burners create their own momentum.
